In March 2026, attorney Paul Lesko filed 15 arbitration demands against Whatnot on behalf of 30 clients, alleging the platform operates an illegal gambling operation through its randomized box breaks and repack breaks.

The claims argue that buying a spot in a randomized break satisfies the three legal elements of gambling: consideration (you pay), chance (a wheel or random assignment determines your team), and prize (the cards you receive). Under California law — where Whatnot is headquartered — that combination may constitute an illegal lottery.

Whatnot has pushed back, stating that card breaks are a long-standing hobby format and that gambling is strictly prohibited on their platform.

## Why This Matters for Break Buyers

Whether or not the legal claims succeed, the underlying concern is real: too many buyers enter breaks without understanding the math.

Industry data suggests that on average, participating in high-end breaks results in a loss of 60-80% of the initial buy-in value over the long term. When you buy a $500 spot, the statistical likelihood is that you receive less than $200 in card value — if anything at all.

That's not a condemnation of breaking. It's a call for honesty about what it is.
